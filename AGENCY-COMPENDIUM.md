## 1. What Is Agency

Agency is the ability to get what you want across different contexts — the general skill of coming up with ambitious goals and actually achieving them, whatever they are.

It is not a fixed trait. It is a learnable skill, built through deliberate practice, mindset shifts, and accumulated wins.

### Working Definitions

**Phil Filippak (Unstable Orbits):**
> "My personal definition of agency is the weighed score of all actions that have paid off in the way they were planned to, with greater weights assigned to those aimed farther into the future."

**Evie Cottrell:**
> "By 'agency' I mean the ability to get what you want across different contexts — the general skill of coming up with ambitious goals and actually achieving them."

**Agencymaxxing manifesto:**
> "You absolutely have to be figuring out how you can increase your capacity to influence your own thoughts and behaviors as well as your faith in your ability to handle a wide range of tasks and situations."

### What Agency Is Not

- It is not dominance over others
- It is not recklessness or recklessness disguised as confidence
- It is not saying yes to everything
- It is not a personality type you are born with

## 2. Why Agency Matters

### Multiplier Effects

When you are agentic, others become more interested in helping you because you are a good investment. You apply a multiplier to others' help so that their contribution goes further than if they gave it to someone who wastes it.

> "People like when their help makes a meaningful difference and they don't like when their help goes to waste."

### Cascading Impact

Agentic people help those around them increase their agency, who then help those around them, and so on. Building agency is not selfish — it creates positive externalities.

### Expanded Possibility Space

> "When you're agencymaxxing, you have a more expansive experience of life because you see more possibilities, and you can only ever pursue things that you recognize as possibilities."

### Historical Precedent

> "When you're agencymaxxing, you participate in a grand tradition carried out by your favorite historical figures. They agencymaxxed their way into the history books."

## 3. Obstacles to Agency

### 3.1 Fear

Fear is the primary enemy. It suppresses options in your decision space. It locks away alternatives in the more distant future.

> "Fear the mind-killer. Fear the little-death. There is no simple recipe to fight off fear, and you probably don't want to get rid of it completely but anyway, fear is only strong around your most sensitive spots. Most of the damage it does is collateral."

Fear is often worse than the thing it protects you from. Deconstructing why something is scary often dispels its detrimental effect because it actually only seems scary.

**How fear limits agency:**
- Each fear disables a move in your decision space
- It caps the subspace of alternatives available in the future
- Freeing one move you've been afraid of unlocks ten moves a year from now and a hundred moves two years from now

### 3.2 Imaginary Rules

You have imposed many rules on yourself that do not actually exist. These invisible constraints are often the heaviest shackles.

> "Stop adding imaginary rules to the things you do." — Charlie T

**Common imaginary rules:**
- "I need permission to do X"
- "I'm not qualified/experienced/impressive enough"
- "That's not for people like me"
- "I should wait until the timing is right"
- "I can't start until I have the perfect plan"

### 3.3 Petty Tyrants

Petty tyrants are those who exercise their power to distract you or make you suffer. They can be people, but also situations, systems, or even messy street light patterns. The key is: never let them get you.

**Identifying petty tyrants:**
- Who or what reliably drains your energy?
- What situations cause you to lose focus or agency?
- Where do you find yourself procrastinating or avoiding?

### 3.4 Social Discouragement

Agency is often socially discouraged in different groups. It can feel shameful — associated with being entitled, "too much," or bossy. This is especially true for women and marginalized groups.

> "I sometimes still have a voice in my head saying, 'who do you think you???' But, fortunately, I'm paying less and less attention to it."

### 3.5 Premature Dismissal

Most people only consider the opportunities in front of them rather than the entire action space of possibilities. They dismiss ideas prematurely and fail to seriously consider what it would look like to deviate from default pathways.

### 3.6 The Permission Trap

It is easy to have an implicit assumption that you need permission to do anything unfamiliar — register a business, apply for a grant, start a project, move across the country. Turns out, you often just don't.

### 3.7 Cultural Speed

> "The downside of being a person is you're kind of weak compared to all the people (culture) but the benefit is you get to have a way faster learning rate. So if you're changing at the same speed as culture, you're going really slow." — Nick Cammarata

## 4. Foundational Layer: Body and Mind

All agentic skills rest on the foundation of basic, elemental awareness. This awareness is grown step by step, not achieved in a quick rush.

### 4.1 Physical Foundation

Strong willpower and sense of control root from the body, not the other way around.

**The 80/20 of physical foundation:**
1. **Healthy diet** — consistent, nutritious, sustainable
2. **Regular exercise** — any movement, done consistently
3. **Good sleep** — the single highest-leverage health intervention

> "Healthy diet, regular exercise, and good sleep will do 80% of the work." — Phil Filippak

### 4.2 Meditation

Three forms of meditation that build agency:

**Form 1: Listen**
- Listen to X, where X can be your breath, your body, the soundscape of your city, a dripping faucet, or the birds singing in the trees

**Form 2: Let things go**
- The first form will provide you with many things you could let go
- Bodily tension is one of those things, and getting rid of it is worth a lot

**Form 3: Wander aimlessly**
- Spend a day without any plan, any intention to do something useful, nothing
- Just sit and watch the clouds go by

### 4.3 Reading Inspiring Fiction

> "Part of the character's agency is yours — if you're ready to absorb it. Well-written heroes never fully fade in your mind, and we can decide whose voice to amplify."

Stories with inspiring characters transfer some of their agency to you. The character's ability to act in the world becomes part of your mental repertoire.

### 4.4 Content Diet

The content you consume can heavily shape your thought patterns. Be intentional about this.

> "I actually think that tech-bro entrepreneurs are great for the purpose of building agency." — Evie Cottrell

You internalize the worldview of the content you binge. Choose authors and creators who model the agency you want to build.

## 5. Awareness and Presence

### 5.1 Expanding Attention

Your attention does not have to be a narrow spotlight. You can expand it and be more aware of your surroundings.

**Benefits of expanded awareness:**
- Greater sense of presence
- Easier time reacting to unforeseen stimuli
- Improved decision-making in complex environments
- Better social awareness

### 5.2 Alexander Technique

The contracted-to-expanded awareness pipeline is often taught as Alexander Technique. Key principle: *"When you stop doing the wrong things, the right thing does itself."*

Example: allowing yourself to walk without imposing your idea of what walking is like.

### 5.3 Spatial Awareness Across Domains

> "Why only expand your awareness in the physical space? You can take any abstract conceptual space and expand it there."

**Practice arenas for expanded awareness:**
- **4X strategy games** — keep track of several spatial, conceptual, and temporal contexts while not allowing yourself to focus on any single one
- **Complex projects** — manage multiple dependencies and timelines simultaneously
- **Social environments** — track multiple conversations, body languages, and group dynamics

### 5.4 The Musashi Principle

Miyamoto Musashi, legendary Japanese swordsman and philosopher, praised the skill of being keenly aware of one's immediate environment as the greatest virtue.

From *The Book of Five Rings*:
- Timing
- Styles of movement
- Honesty
- Environmental awareness

### 5.5 Death as Advisor

> "Be aware of the fact that you may die the next second, and you will become invincible."

This is the ultimate sharpening of awareness. Not an easy path, but it strips away everything non-essential immediately.

## 6. Mindset Shifts

### 6.1 You Are Allowed to Do More

> "You are allowed to do many more things than you think. Conversely, you are allowed not to do many things (honestly, almost all)."

Feel free to abandon past commitments if they don't serve your current goals. There is no achievement for a perfect track record.

### 6.2 Change Faster Than Culture

Your individual learning rate is much higher than culture's. If you change at the same speed as culture, you're going really slow. Use your advantage.

### 6.3 Seeking Forgiveness, Not Permission

Most things you think require permission do not. Just do things. If institutions have a problem with it, seek forgiveness later.

> "If you're not doing harm, you probably don't need to assume that you must seek permission, in the absence of clear evidence that you do."

**Important caveat:** This applies to bureaucratic institutions, not interpersonal relationships. Always respect others' boundaries.

### 6.4 The Upper Bound of Agency

> "You are only ever as agentic as your political theory says is the upper bound of rational agency." — Andrés Gómez Emilsson

What is the most agentic thing you can imagine? Your answer reveals your values and how you set goals. A cooperative frame manifests a much more ambitious and agentic variant of the future.

### 6.5 Asking Dumb Questions

Being unafraid to look stupid is a truly formidable quality.

> "Most people are not willing to do this — looking stupid takes courage, and sometimes it's easier to just let things slide. It is striking how many situations I am in where I start asking basic questions, feel guilty for slowing the group down, and it turns out that nobody understood what was going on to begin with."

This is a habit. It is easy to pick up. And it makes you smarter.

### 6.6 Widening the Option Space

**Two rare and valuable traits:**
1. Considering wide action spaces
2. Taking weird ideas seriously

Most people only consider the opportunities in front of them. They dismiss ideas prematurely. The agentic person asks: *"What is the upper bound scenario?"*

**Exercise:** When facing a decision, force yourself to generate 10 options, including at least 3 that feel "too crazy." Then seriously consider one of the crazy ones.

## 7. Tactical Habits

### 7.1 The "Do It 100 Times" Framework

> "Do it 100 times. Write 100 songs, cook 100 omelettes, talk to 100 people. It never seems like a huge deal until you try it yourself. It's manageable, and yet it stretches you, and you'll be observably different at the end of it." — Visakan Veerasamy

**Rules:**
- No deadlines
- No other modifiers
- Just do it. 100 times.
- Track your count

### 7.2 Behavioral Frameworks

Month-long challenges, personal commitments, structured streaks. Any framework that creates a container for consistent action works.

**Examples:**
- Inktober (daily drawing)
- NaNoWriMo (daily writing)
- 30-day fitness challenges
- "Do it 100 times" challenge
- Daily cold shower
- Daily journaling

### 7.3 Cross-Pollination of Concepts

Learn and mix concepts from a variety of fields. Apply patterns from software architecture to how you schedule your day, or machine learning concepts to your understanding of agency.

**Benefits:**
- Novel solutions to old problems
- Increased eloquence with complex ideas
- Ability to communicate across domain boundaries

### 7.4 Thinking About Agency

Simply thinking about agency increases it. Reflecting on your own agency, reading about it, and discussing it all raise your baseline.

> "Don't overdo it though." — Phil Filippak

Thinking without acting is the trap. Reflection should lead to action.

### 7.5 Intentional Content Consumption

Listen to hundreds of hours of content from agentic people and you will internalize their worldview — asking people for stuff, putting yourself out there, not asking for permission.

**Curated content accelerates agency acquisition.**

## 8. Social Leverage

### 8.1 Outsourcing Agency

As paradoxical as it may sound, you can outsource some of your agency. Alcoholics Anonymous is a perfect example — you use the power of the community to set yourself straight.

> "A body of people is often helpful when you need to get things done."

**Mechanisms:**
- Accountability partners
- Mastermind groups
- Communities of practice
- Mentorship
- Peer pressure (positive)

### 8.2 Joining Multiple Communities

Join communities with wildly different agendas. Each group's preferences will tug yours in some direction which may help your set of beliefs become more elaborate and robust.

> "The solution to cults is to get involved in more of them, not avoid them." — Tiago Forte

### 8.3 Asking for Help

There will (nearly) always be people who can help you achieve your goals better. Find them and ask them.

> "If you don't ask, the answer is always no."

**Having a low bar for asking is a learnable mindset.**

Whenever you encounter something difficult or aversive:
1. Ask: "Who would be the best person to have on my side here?"
2. Reach out to people who could help
3. Be explicit about your intent: *"I'm experimenting with asking for more favours and being more accepting of rejections."*

**Barriers to asking and solutions:**

| Barrier | Solution |
|---------|----------|
| Hard to know what you want | "If this was easy, what would it look like?" |
| Hard to imagine how others help | "Whose advice would help me solve this?" |
| Intimidating to ask | Remember: EAs (and most smart people) love agency |
| Vulnerable to rejection | Gamify rejection (see Section 10) |

### 8.4 Accountability Systems

Sending daily plans to an accountability partner and receiving feedback is extremely powerful. It creates a lightweight structure that supports consistency.

## 9. Strategic Agency

### 9.1 The Agency Pipeline

Phil Filippak proposes a three-layer pipeline:

```
Fundamental → Tactical → Strategic
```

**Fundamental:** Body, awareness, meditation, fear management
**Tactical:** Daily habits, frameworks, community, asking
**Strategic:** Long-term vision, political theory of agency, compounding awareness

### 9.2 Fear and the Decision Space

All the things fear suppresses but not targets can be mapped to points in your decision space. The more of them are disabled, the weaker your ability to maneuver.

**The compounding effect:**
- Freeing one move you've been afraid of
- Unlocks ten moves a year from now
- Unlocks a hundred moves two years from now

It is unrealistic for a human to exhaust the alternative timelines mine. There is always more room to expand.

### 9.3 Spatial Awareness as Strategy

Expand your awareness from physical space to abstract conceptual spaces.

**Practice environments:**
- 4X strategy games (low-stakes)
- Complex work projects (medium-stakes)
- Life decisions (high-stakes)

### 9.4 The Political Theory of Agency

Your agency is bounded by what you believe is possible for yourself. If your worldview caps agency at a low level, you will never exceed that cap.

Ask yourself:
- What is the most agentic thing I can imagine?
- How does it relate to ambition?
- What is the most ambitious endeavor I could undertake?
- Do I believe in a world of benevolent cooperators or a world where I must strive alone?

The setup involving a lot of cooperation manifests a much more ambitious and agentic variant of the future.

## 10. The Rejection Framework

### 10.1 Why Gamify Rejection

If you are going to put yourself out there, you are going to get rejected. The solution is not to put yourself out there less, but to learn to get a kick out of rejections.

### 10.2 The Rejection Game

**Setup:**
1. Find a friend or stranger who also wants to become unstoppable
2. Create a shared Google doc
3. Commit to writing all of your rejections in it for a fixed period
4. Decide a prize for the winner and agree to constraints
5. Whoever has the most rejections by the end wins

### 10.3 Why This Works

**1. Rejections are evidence of exploration**
If you're not being rejected from anything, you're not properly exploring the boundaries of your option space. If you were accepted to everything, you clearly weren't exploring enough. In expectation, you were missing out on valuable opportunities.

**2. More applications = more acceptances**
If you apply to more stuff, you'll likely get more acceptances as well as more rejections.

**3. Lower psychological cost**
Being comfortable with rejection removes a fear that normally prevents you from proactively seeking opportunities. Decouple rejection and shame.

**4. It becomes fun**
> "Lately, rejections have felt almost thrilling. This is because it feeds into the narrative that I am becoming the person I want to be."

**5. Romantic resilience bonus**
It strengthens your resilience to romantic rejections as well.

### 10.4 The Mindset Shift

When rejected, use it to self-signal that you are becoming the unstoppable version of yourself. Update your model of the world, but do not take emotional damage or conclude you are deficient.

> "When I imagine the most unstoppable/confident/agentic version of myself, it's not that I am getting accepted to everything. Instead, I am pushing myself and exploring my limits."

### 10.5 Rejection as Empowerment

> "External circumstances have less and less control over me."

Each rejection that does not break you makes you more resilient. You are training yourself to decouple your self-worth from external validation.

## 11. Serendipity Engineering

### 11.1 What Is a Serendipity Vehicle

A serendipity vehicle is a thing that is out there in the world that allows you to manufacture serendipitous events. It is something you put into the world that creates opportunities for chance encounters and unexpected connections.

**Key insight:** It is often hard to see the explicit benefits beforehand. Putting yourself out there doesn't have explicit CV points.

### 11.2 How to Increase Serendipity Surface Area

**Low-bar sharing:**
- Write a blog post when you're confused about an idea
- Share what you're working on publicly
- Post your questions, not just your answers

**Low-bar outreach:**
- If you read a blog post that resonated, reach out to the author
- Send cold emails to people you admire
- Use Twitter/online platforms actively

**Strategic presence:**
- Be in rooms where interesting things happen
- Attend events outside your field
- Create things that can travel without you

### 11.3 Cold Emails and Social Media

> "Cold emails and Twitter are a godsend for people who have high potential, but lack the opportunity to realise it. A few emails or tweets to a person you don't know can literally change your life."

> "If you can demonstrate that you have high potential and/or can be useful to somebody, you should just email/tweet them and let them know about it. If you're thinking 'well, I'm not impressive enough' you're likely wrong." — Alexey Guzey

### 11.4 Compounding Serendipity

One conversation can drastically improve the trajectory of your life. You cannot predict when these moments will occur, but you can increase the rate at which they happen.

Think of serendipity engineering as increasing the probability surface of life-changing encounters per unit of time.

## 12. Sustainability and Compounding

### 12.1 Sustainable Growth Over Intensity

> "When you're agencymaxxing, you recognize that increasing your agency in a steady and sustainable manner is better than hyperincreasing your agency and subsequently burning out, decreasing your agency."

**The burnout trap:**
- Sprint hard → burn out → lose all gains → restart from lower baseline
- Slow and steady → compound → exponential curve over months

### 12.2 The Compound Interest of Agency

Every small win builds confidence for the next. Every rejection survived builds resilience for the next. Every skill acquired opens doors to new skills.

Agency compounds because:
- Each free move unlocks more future moves
- Each new connection creates more potential connections
- Each fear conquered makes the next fear smaller
- Each boundary pushed redefines what feels normal

### 12.3 The Quarterly Review

Periodically zoom out and assess:
- What expanded your agency most?
- What was noise?
- Which habits are serving you?
- Which should be dropped?
- What phase should you focus on next?

### 12.4 The Long Game

Agency is not a project with an end date. It is a way of being that you refine over a lifetime. The goal is not to "achieve agency" but to become more agentic over time.

> "I wish you to be ever increasingly alive and agentic!" — Phil Filippak
